Depreciation Trap

Cars lose ~15% value yearly. Putting all your cash into a depreciating asset (Strategy 1) destroys wealth. Keeping cash (Strategy 2) lets it grow.

Arbitrage Math

If your loan costs 9% but your investments earn 13%, you make a "profit" of 4% on the bank's money. This is how the rich use debt.

Liquidity is King

In Strategy 2, you have cash in Mutual Funds. In an emergency, you can sell it in 2 days. In Strategy 1, your money is stuck in the car's metal.

What This Calculator Ignores

Real life has more variables. We focused on the loan math, but don't forget these costs:

  • Maintenance & Repairs Luxury cars cost exponentially more to service as they age (Years 5-7).
  • Insurance Depreciation Premiums might drop, but claim ratios often increase with age. Zero-dep cover gets expensive.
  • Fuel & Running Costs We ignored fuel. If you drive >1500km/month, an EV might change this math entirely.
