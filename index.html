Depreciation Trap

A car loses ~15% value yearly. Your Total Net Worth view above now includes this loss, showing why keeping cash (which grows) helps offset the car (which shrinks).

Arbitrage Math

If your loan costs 9% but your investments earn 13%, you make a "profit" of 4% on the bank's money. This is how the rich use debt.

Real Cost of Ownership

Notice the "With Fuel" line? Fuel often adds 50% on top of your EMI. Always budget for this hidden cost.

Other Factors to Consider

Real life has more variables. We focused on the loan math, but don't forget these:

  • Maintenance Costs Luxury cars cost exponentially more to service as they age (Years 5-7).
  • Insurance Premiums Premiums might drop, but claim ratios often increase with age. Zero-dep cover gets expensive.
